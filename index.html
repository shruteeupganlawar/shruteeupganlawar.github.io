// 01 — about

Who I Am

I'm a manufacturing engineer turned roboticist — one who's worked across varied domains, from automobile assembly lines at Bajaj Auto Ltd. and Mercedes-Benz, researching aircraft components and 3D printing at Skyroot Aerospace, to working on communication protocols in robotic systems and using LLMs for zero-shot trajectory generation at the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore.

My aim lies in giving robots a nervous system — the ability to understand the world they inhabit — building machines that perceive, reason, and adapt in real time. I aspire to dive deep into SLAM, motion planning, robot cognition, and perception to build autonomous systems that navigate and reason in unstructured environments.

Beyond research and engineering, I've led from the front — as Secretary of TEDxCOEP, curating the most talks in the club's history, and as a student representative on the Board of Studies shaping the undergraduate curriculum for Manufacturing Science and Engineering at COEP. I believe the same systems thinking that makes a good roboticist makes a good leader: define the problem, align the team, iterate.

In my world parallel with robotics, you can find me writing poems, reflections, musings, exploring nerdy topics (especially astronomy) or dancing!
